Slide 2

Nonprofits waste too many hours finding and filling out grants.

The process is manual at both ends: discovery and application.

Finding grants

  • Teams sift through long grant lists to figure out what is actually relevant.
  • There is no fast way to personalize search to the organization’s mission and profile.

Preparing applications

  • Important information is buried across PDFs, tax forms, annual reports, and incorporation docs.
  • The same facts are re-entered again and again in different formats.

Operational cost

  • Small nonprofits often do not have dedicated grant staff.
  • Every avoidable hour spent on grants is time taken away from the mission.
Slide 3

Why ChatGPT or Claude alone is not enough

General-purpose AI helps with writing. It does not solve the full grant workflow.

No discovery layer

A chatbot does not help teams quickly identify which grants are actually personalized to their organization.

No workflow memory

Users still have to upload documents manually, re-prompt, and copy-paste answers across forms.

No in-portal execution

A chatbot does not sit inside grant portals and safely autofill fields where the work actually happens.

Slide 4

GrantFlow is a grant workflow product, not just a writing tool.

We reduce time at both stages: finding the right opportunities and completing the application.

1

Upload documents

2

3

Find personalized grants

The platform helps surface grants that better match the organization’s profile, mission, and likely eligibility.

4

Autofill applications

The extension fills repetitive factual fields and drafts longer answers grounded in uploaded documents.

Slide 5

The core wedge: personalized grant discovery + application acceleration

Most tools focus on drafting. GrantFlow solves the higher-value workflow before and after drafting.

Before the application

  • Document upload: centralize nonprofit materials in one place.
  • Profile generation: build a reusable organizational context layer.
  • Grant search: narrow down grants based on who the organization is, not just keyword search.

During the application

  • Field detection: identify relevant fields across grant portals.
  • Safe autofill: fill repetitive factual data only when confidence is strong.
  • Document-grounded drafting: generate longer responses using the nonprofit’s own materials.
